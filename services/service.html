INTERVIEW PREPARATION

ROUND1: APTITUDE TRAINING

The aptitude test usually consists of multiple-choice questions that test a candidate's verbal and quantitative abilities. The verbal section assesses a candidate's language skills, including reading comprehension, grammar, and vocabulary. The quantitative section tests a candidate's numerical and analytical skills, including basic mathematical operations, data interpretation, and logical reasoning.

ROUND2: CODING TRAINING

A coding round is a common component of technical assessments used by companies to evaluate a candidate's programming skills. In a coding round, candidates are typically presented with a set of problems or tasks that they must solve using programming languages such as Java, Python, or C++.The tasks can range from simple algorithms and data structures to more complex problems that require knowledge of specific libraries and frameworks. Candidates are usually given a fixed amount of time to complete the tasks, and their solutions are evaluated based on factors such as correctness, efficiency, and readability.

ROUND3: HR INTERVIEW

HR round, also known as the "personal interview" or "HR interview," is the final stage of the job interview process. This is usually conducted by the company's Human Resources (HR) department, and its purpose is to assess the candidate's personality, character, communication skills, work ethics, and compatibility with the company culture. During the HR round, the interviewer may ask questions about the candidate's personal background, hobbies, interests, and expectations from the job.
